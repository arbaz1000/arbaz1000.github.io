Forecasting Non-Stationary Time-Series

Master's Thesis at IIT Bombay | Industry collaboration with SigmaStream LLC
Published as Indian Patent Application

The Problem

  • Real-world time-series (drilling sensors, cardiac monitors, financial instruments) are non-stationary — their statistical properties shift as the system moves between operating regimes
  • Conventional models (ARIMA, exponential smoothing) assume stationarity. When the regime changes, they fail silently
  • The harder problem: automatically detect regime changes and adapt the forecast, without a human in the loop

High-Level Design

  • Transform time-series segments into time-frequency images (CWT scalograms)
  • Extract visual features via a pretrained CNN — no training on domain data
  • Cluster the feature vectors — each cluster = a distinct regime
  • Train a separate forecasting model per regime
  • At inference: identify the current regime, route to the right model, forecast
Two-phase pipeline for regime-aware forecasting

Low-Level Design

Stage Detail
Segmentation Overlapping sliding windows. Overlap ensures no regime transition is missed at boundaries.
Time-Freq Transform Morlet CWT at multiple scales. Produces a 2D scalogram per segment. Unlike STFT, CWT gives adaptive resolution across frequencies.
Feature Extraction ResNet50 pretrained on ImageNet. No fine-tuning — learned visual features generalize to scalograms.
Dim. Reduction UMAP to 2D. Preserves local + global structure. t-SNE would merge distinct regimes.
Clustering GMM with automatic k via BIC maximization. No manual regime count needed.
Regime Mapping Each sample mapped to its cluster. Contiguous same-cluster stretches form regime segments.
Forecasting Per-cluster ARMA(p,q). Trend removed per segment. p, q selected via AIC.
Model Refresh Phase 1 re-runs periodically. Cluster boundaries and ARMA parameters adapt to distributional drift.

Results

  • Regime-specific ARMA consistently outperformed global ARIMA baselines, especially at regime transition boundaries where single-model approaches fail silently
  • Framework validated across domains: oil drilling, biomedical ECG, synthetic multi-regime signals
  • Published as Indian Patent Application

Applied: Rig State Detection

Solution architecture under NDA. Problem and results shared with permission.

Problem

  • During oil-well drilling, 10 surface sensors (bit depth, hole depth, WOB, hook load, flow rate, RPM, torque, pump pressure, block height, on/off bottom) stream data at 1 Hz to a monitoring dashboard
  • The rig cycles through 10 macro operational states — stationary, cementing, tripping in, tripping out, casing, linear, tubing, drilling, out of surface, conditioning
  • These states need to be detected and marked on the dashboard in near real-time to enable operational decision-making and downstream analytics

Outcome

97%+ — classification accuracy across all 5 wells, 10 operational states from 10 surface sensors
  • Near real-time downhole state detection on streaming surface sensor data
  • Validated as part of an industry collaboration with SigmaStream LLC

Cross-Domain Validation

  • The core technique (CWT scalogram → pretrained CNN → downstream task) was independently validated on biomedical signal processing — fetal QRS complex detection from abdominal ECG using CWT + VGG16
92%+ precision, 100% recall — QRS complex detection on cardiac signals
  • Confirms the approach generalizes beyond drilling and industrial time-series
