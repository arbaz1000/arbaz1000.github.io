Workwise AI

Procurement & Estimation Platform
Role: Solo AI/backend engineer — problem framing, domain research, solution design, shipping, feedback loops

Infrastructure procurement is a long chain — tenders get published, companies decide whether to bid, BOQs get broken into vendor inquiries, quotes come back, costs get estimated, bids get submitted, and then the tendering authority evaluates everything. Most of this chain runs on manual document work. I built the AI systems that automate each step.

System Map

Two personas, one platform — shared product intelligence underneath

How It Fits Together

The Tendering Authority side is straightforward — after vendors submit bids, the platform runs automated Technical Evaluation (compliance checking, scoring) and Financial Evaluation (price comparison, loading factors, ranking). What took a committee days of manual review produces structured reports in minutes.

The EPC Company side is where the complexity lives. An EPC receives a tender with 10–30 documents and needs to decide whether to bid, then execute the entire procurement chain if they do:

  • Tender Summary extracts scope, timelines, eligibility, and commercial terms from the full tender package — the team makes a go/no-go decision without reading hundreds of pages
  • BOQ-to-RFQ is the hardest step — interpreting each BOQ line item requires domain expertise that traditionally bottlenecks on experienced engineers. The pipeline uses semantic chunking, a scratchpad reasoning step, and structured extraction to produce vendor-ready RFQ packages with matched products, spec sheets, and approved makes
  • RFQs go out to vendors. Quotation Parsing (WIP) will extract structured data from returned quotes and compare against RFQ requirements — flagging matches, deviations, and missing products
  • Cost Estimation (WIP) will aggregate parsed quotes with historical pricing to produce the final bid estimate

Everything above depends on the Product & Vendor Database — 7,000+ deduplicated products with structured specs and 20,000+ vendor associations. An LLM pipeline classifies, extracts, normalizes, and deduplicates products from any source. Catalog Parsing handles the upstream bottleneck: turning vendor PDF catalogs into structured data the pipeline can ingest. EPC companies also use Tech Eval + Financial Eval to evaluate their own vendors’ bids — same system, different context.
